# Weapon Types

This is an overview of weapon styles and the vocabulary surrounding them. I will go more in depth on weapon design in a future page, but this is just a reference for how to describe the robots you'll see around in competitions.

## Vertical Spinners

Vertical spinners are weapons that spin perpendicular to the ground. Typically, they spin "upward" or towards themselves in the direction where they can hit the underside of the opponent in order to pop them off the ground (or roof shot them).

### Beater Bar

Beater bars/eggbeaters are a wide, rectangular vertical spinning weapons. They take advantage of their high coverage and optimal moment of inertia.

### Drum

Drum spinners are a similar to beater bars, but cylindrical with teeth that stick out. They also benefit from wide hitting area.

### Bar
Bars are (relatively) thinner single-blade weapons that are characteristically sheet cut. Bars are longer or "bar" shaped.

</p>

### Disk

Disks, like bars, are thinner single-blade weapons that are sheet cut. The difference is that disks are typically round with a tooth/point, as opposed to bar shaped.

### Drisk

Drisks are two disks fastened together to have more bite coverage.
