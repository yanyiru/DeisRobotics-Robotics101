# Weapon Types

This is an overview of weapon styles and the vocabulary surrounding them. I will go more in depth on weapon design in a future page, but this is just a reference for how to describe the robots you'll see around in competitions.

## Vertical Spinners

Vertical spinners are weapons that spin perpendicular to the ground. Typically, they spin "upward" or towards themselves in the direction where they can hit the underside of the opponent in order to pop them off the ground (or roof shot them).

### Beater Bar

Beater bars/eggbeaters are a wide, rectangular vertical spinning weapons. They take advantage of their high coverage and optimal moment of inertia.

## Horizontal Spinners

Horizontal spinners are weapons that spin parallel to the ground. They are mainly distinguished by where on the robot they are mounted. Horizontal weapons are typically either shaped like a disk or a bar.

### Full Body Spinner

Full body spinners are a bit of a category within itself, but are characterized by a circular shape where a significant of their reachable body being part of the weapon. These styles include ring spinners, which are pancake-shaped and have the weapon around the side and shell spinners, which are dome shaped with the entire outer dome spinning.

Hammersaw bots have a spinning disk at the end of the arm with a tooth. They use a fast and forceful swing to imbed the tooth of the disk into the top of their opponent or cut their parts.

## Control Bots

Control bots include a number of sub-archetypes, including flippers, lifters, and wedges. They focus on using excellent driving and geometry to inhibit the opponent's driving and avoid damage, gaining points through the control criteria.
